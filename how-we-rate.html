How We Rate and Review Operators

Editorial Team · Review Methodology Last updated: 12 June 2026

Star ratings only mean something if the process behind them is consistent, documented and independent. This page explains exactly how each score on this site is produced.

Image placeholder — rating-methodology (1024x1024)

Our review methodology

Each review is carried out by a single named analyst over a minimum of two weeks, using a real, identity-verified account funded with our own money. The analyst follows a fixed checklist of more than sixty test points, from registration friction to the wording of bonus clauses, and keeps timestamped notes that editors can audit.

We never accept pre-written copy from operators, and operators do not see reviews before publication.

Rating categories and weighting

The overall star rating is a weighted average of six category scores. Payments and trust carry the most weight because they matter most when something goes wrong.

CategoryWeightWhat we measure
Account experience25%Account record verification, corporate transparency, RG tools
Payments25%Real deposit/withdrawal tests, limits, fees, speed
Bonus fairness15%Wagering levels, expiry, restrictive clauses
Sportsbook product15%Market depth, odds margins, live tools
Casino product10%Catalogue breadth, providers, published RTPs
Customer support10%Response times, answer quality, channel coverage

Testing accounts, deposits and withdrawals

Every operator is tested with at least three deposits across different methods and at least one full withdrawal, including the identity-verification step. The processing times printed in our payment tables are what we actually experienced — not what the operator advertises.

  • Deposits tested: card, bank transfer and at least one e-wallet
  • Withdrawal measured from request to funds received
  • Support contacted with at least three genuine queries
  • Responsible-gambling limits activated and verified on the live account

Editorial independence and affiliate disclosure

This site is funded through affiliate commissions: when a reader clicks through to an operator and registers, we may be paid. That relationship never changes a score, the order of recommendations, or our willingness to publish negative findings. Analysts are paid salaries, not per-signup commissions, and the methodology above applies identically to partners and non-partners.

Disclosure summary

We may receive a commission from operators featured on this site. Scores and rankings are determined solely by the documented testing process. Content is informational; it is not an inducement to gamble. .

How often reviews are updated

Reviews carry a "last updated" date and are re-tested on a rolling schedule: a full re-test at least every twelve months and a rules-and-payments check every six. We also re-open a review immediately whenever a product status, bonus structure or payment line-up changes, or when reader reports flag a discrepancy.
